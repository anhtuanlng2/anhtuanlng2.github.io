ABOUT US

GiftVN was started in Singapore in January 2016, by a very busy mom named Theresa. Theresa has 3 young children and spends far too much time shopping for birthday gifts for her children’s friends and her friends’ kids. She realized how much time and effort goes into all that – and only to notice that many gifts get duplicated, tossed aside, or are simply not appreciated! Amid all these birthday parties, she envisioned a way that gift-giving would be meaningful for the recipient, simple for the giver, healthy for the environment, and beneficial to local community.

CREAT AN EVEN

A Gift party makes a difference. You could have had any other kind of party, but you have decided to do something extra special and help someone else in need – that’s something to celebrate! As I’ve always tried to tell my kids, giving is just as important as getting, and by celebrating with Gift-It-Forward, you are making a difference to someone’s life.

We want our kids to grow up kind. We also want our kids to be grateful for what they have, and the greatest gift that parents can teach their children is generosity itself. By becoming role models themselves, parents can inspire their children to give back while enhancing their own well-being. Teaching your kids the spirit of giving while highlighting the importance of helping the less-privileged goes a long way towards helping them grow into productive and kind members of society. Here are 4 reasons why and how you ought to share the spirit of giving with your kids

Kids who engage in acts of givings, as well as acts of volunteerism, are more likely to have a greater respect for others, possess stronger leadership skills, and have a better understanding of citizenship that carries over into adulthood. Moreover, as volunteering connects you to others, your children will be able to develop strong interpersonal skills, allow them to make new friends and boost their self-esteem and confidence. How you can get your kids to be interested in volunteering is by highlighting how good it feels to volunteer. You can do this by describing the personal satisfaction that comes from helping others and involving your kids in your giving decisions.
